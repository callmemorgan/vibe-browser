# Public Roadmap

## Purpose

The public roadmap shows where Vibe Browser Benchmark is going without
promising dates or leaking hidden-test plans. It should help contributors,
participants, and readers understand priorities and upcoming compatibility
breaks.

## Roadmap Lanes

- benchmark methodology
- milestone checkers
- harness support
- scoring and evaluator calibration
- public data exports
- WPT, fuzzing, and visual evidence
- website and leaderboard UX
- governance and submission review
- reproducibility infrastructure

## Entry Fields

Each roadmap item includes title, lane, status, public summary, target season
or release if known, affected specs, expected compatibility impact, owner,
decision link, and last-updated date.

## Statuses

Use `exploring`, `planned`, `in-progress`, `blocked`, `shipped`,
`deferred`, and `withdrawn`. A roadmap item is not a commitment until a
release note, benchmark card, or governance decision makes it binding.

## Hidden-Test Safety

Roadmap entries may describe classes of future checks but must not reveal
hidden-test inputs, exact fixtures, or exploitable scoring details.

## Research Basis

W3C-style public process norms motivate visible work items, review state, and
decision traceability. Keep a Changelog motivates linking shipped roadmap work
to concrete release notes.

## QA Pass

Checked against `governance-and-versioning.md`, `release-notes-template.md`,
`benchmark-card.md`, `public-homepage.md`, and hidden-test backlog scope. This
spec defines public roadmap content, not internal project management.
