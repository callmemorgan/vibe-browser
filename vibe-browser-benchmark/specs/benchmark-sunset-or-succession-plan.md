# Benchmark Sunset or Succession Plan

## Purpose

The sunset or succession plan defines what happens if Vibe Browser Benchmark
becomes obsolete, saturated, unmaintained, or replaced by a better benchmark.

## Sunset Triggers

- sustained saturation
- hidden-test compromise that cannot be repaired
- low reproducibility
- maintainer capacity loss
- better successor benchmark
- task no longer representative
- unresolved legal or safety risk
- community trust collapse

## Sunset Process

Maintainers publish proposal, collect feedback, announce final season, archive
data, preserve citations, freeze scoring changes, and document successor path.

## Succession

A successor benchmark should define migration notes, comparable and
non-comparable concepts, data reuse rules, domain transfer if applicable, and
how historical Vibe Browser results remain discoverable.

## Preservation

Retired public pages remain available as archives or tombstones. Hidden tests
may be released only when no longer harmful and after governance review.

## Research Basis

DataCite tombstone guidance motivates clear landing pages for unavailable
citable objects. Digital preservation guidance motivates maintaining access and
context after active operations end.

## QA Pass
