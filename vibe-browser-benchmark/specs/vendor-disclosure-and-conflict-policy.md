# Vendor Disclosure and Conflict Policy

## Purpose

Vendor disclosure and conflict policy ensures public readers can see when a
result, report, run, or benchmark decision may be influenced by a vendor or
financial relationship.

## Disclosure Fields

- submitter organization
- sponsor relationship
- paid hosted run
- maintainer affiliation
- evaluator conflict
- report sponsorship
- infrastructure donation
- model-provider relationship
- embargo request
- reviewer recusal

## Conflict Handling

Conflicted maintainers and evaluators must recuse from scoring or appeal
decisions where independence is required. Vendor-submitted results need
independent certification review before official ranking.

## Public Labels

Run pages, leaderboards, reports, and exports show conflict labels in compact
form with a link to the detailed disclosure record.

## Enforcement

Undisclosed conflicts can trigger correction, re-review, official-unranked
status, or incident response depending on impact.

## Research Basis

ACM conflict-of-interest policy motivates early identification and management
of relationships that could affect judgment. NIST AI RMF governance guidance
motivates accountability and transparency for evaluation systems.
