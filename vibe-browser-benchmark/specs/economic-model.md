# Economic Model

## Purpose

The economic model defines how the benchmark is funded, how paid services are
handled, and how financial relationships are disclosed without affecting
scoring decisions.

## Funding Modes

- unpaid community project
- sponsored infrastructure
- paid hosted runs
- grants
- vendor-sponsored reports
- consulting or support services
- donations

## Disclosure Requirements

Public pages disclose sponsors, paid submissions, queue-priority rules,
report sponsorship, maintainer affiliations, and any financial relationship
with vendors whose results are published.

## Firewall Rules

Payment can affect operational services only where disclosed, such as hosted
run capacity. Payment must not change scoring, hidden-test access, evaluator
standards, certification gates, or correction handling.

## Pricing And Access

If hosted runs are paid, publish pricing, included resources, refund policy,
queue expectations, and whether equivalent self-run submission remains
available.

## Research Basis

ACM conflict-of-interest policy motivates identifying and managing conflicts
before publication decisions. NIST AI RMF governance guidance motivates
transparent accountability for AI evaluation systems.
