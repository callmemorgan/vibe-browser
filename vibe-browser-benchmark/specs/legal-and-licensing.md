# Legal and Licensing

## Purpose

Legal and licensing define the baseline terms for benchmark docs, data,
generated submissions, artifacts, reports, and third-party materials. The goal
is clarity, not custom legal complexity.

## License Areas

- benchmark source and harness code
- documentation and methodology text
- public data exports
- reports and charts
- submitted artifacts
- screenshots and generated media
- third-party specs and fixtures
- dependency metadata

## Required Disclosures

Public pages identify license, attribution requirement, warranty disclaimer,
redistribution limits, third-party notices, and takedown contact. Data exports
include machine-readable license metadata.

## Submitted Artifacts

Submission terms must say what rights the benchmark needs to store, verify,
redact, publish, and archive artifacts. Submitters remain responsible for
ensuring they may redistribute dependencies and generated code.

## Takedown

Maintain a process for copyright, privacy, malware, and secret-leak takedowns.
Takedowns preserve tombstone metadata where safe so citations do not break
without explanation.

## Research Basis

Creative Commons guidance motivates clear attribution and license notices for
shared materials. DataCite and FAIR practices motivate license metadata for
reusable datasets.
