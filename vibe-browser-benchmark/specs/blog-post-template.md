# Blog Post Template

## Purpose

The blog post template standardizes result announcements, benchmark releases,
analysis posts, and correction notes so public communication stays careful,
evidence-backed, and reproducible.

## Required Structure

1. **Title**: literal result, release, or analysis claim with season/track when
   relevant.
2. **Dek**: one sentence summarizing why the post matters.
3. **Status box**: season, date, author, run IDs, official/provisional state,
   and links to methodology/data.
4. **Headline result**: scoped claim and top-line chart/table.
5. **Method recap**: profiles, prompts, scoring, evaluator status, and hidden
   check boundary.
6. **What changed or surprised us**: qualitative analysis and representative
   evidence.
7. **Failure modes and caveats**: limitations, non-comparable runs, uncertainty,
   and provider drift.
8. **Reproduce or inspect**: links to result cards, artifacts, exports, and
   commands where public.
9. **What comes next**: upcoming season, methodology change, or open question.
10. **Corrections policy**: how updates will be appended.

## Claim Rules

Use scoped language such as "top official run in season X, track Y." Do not
claim general model superiority, full browser conformance, or production
readiness unless a separate analysis supports it.

## Chart Rules

Charts must name season, track, score definition, sample count, and whether
non-comparable runs are included. Every chart links to the underlying data
export.

## Research Basis

MLCommons motivates public releases grounded in reproducibility and clear
submission rules. Leaderboard-operations research motivates transparency,
documentation, and accountability for public benchmark communication.

## QA Pass
