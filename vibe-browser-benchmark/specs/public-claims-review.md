# Public Claims Review

## Purpose

Public claims review defines how benchmark-derived claims are checked before
appearing in official posts, reports, badges, vendor pages, or press material.

## Claims Covered

- top result
- frontier result
- state-of-the-art wording
- model comparison
- harness comparison
- cost or efficiency claim
- human comparison
- security or conformance implication
- vendor quote
- badge or embed copy

## Review Checklist

Each claim must identify season, track, comparison group, official status,
evidence link, data export, uncertainty, conflict labels, and caveats. Claims
that exceed the evidence are revised or rejected.

## Third-Party Claims

Vendors and external users may cite public results, but must preserve official
labels, citation links, and caveats. Misleading use can trigger correction or
takedown request.

## Public Record

Official claims link to reviewed source data. Corrections append history and
connect to the original claim.

## Research Basis

NIST AI RMF transparency guidance motivates clear communication of AI evaluation
limits. DataCite citation practice motivates stable references for public
claims based on datasets.
