# Onboarding for Submitters

## Purpose

Onboarding for submitters helps participants produce complete, safe, and
comparable submissions before they enter official review.

## Required Guide Sections

- benchmark overview
- choosing season, track, and division
- local dry run
- provider and harness setup
- artifact expectations
- dependency and intervention disclosure
- eligibility checklist
- privacy and secret hygiene
- common failure modes
- external submission packet
- what happens after submission
- appeal and correction paths

## Preflight Checklist

Submitters should verify clean baseline, lockfiles, run summary, public-check
output, artifact size, redaction-safe logs, model identity, harness version, and
comparison profile before upload.

## Support Boundaries

Maintainers can answer public process questions. They must not provide hidden
test clues, private scoring advice, or vendor-specific optimization guidance.

## Research Basis

MLCommons submission guidance motivates explicit prerequisites, artifacts, and
submission categories. Open-source contribution practice motivates clear
quickstarts, expectations, and support boundaries.
