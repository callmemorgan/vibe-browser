# Ethics and Disclosure

## Purpose

Ethics and disclosure define what the benchmark must reveal so readers do not
misinterpret results. The benchmark should reward transparency, not inflated
claims.

## Required Disclosures

- benchmark scope and intended use
- known limitations and threats to validity
- public versus hidden check boundary
- model, provider, and harness identity
- provider drift and mutable model aliases
- vendor-submitted and maintainer-submitted results
- conflicts of interest
- sponsorship or paid-run relationships
- intervention and assistance policy
- artifact redaction and retention limits
- contamination risk
- evaluator subjectivity and uncertainty

## Vendor And Maintainer Results

Vendor-submitted, sponsor-submitted, or maintainer-submitted results can be
official only when conflicts are disclosed and review independence is recorded.
Conflict labels should appear on run pages, reports, and data exports.

## Responsible Interpretation

Public copy must not frame benchmark rank as general intelligence, production
browser readiness, security assurance, or employability. Claims must stay
within season, track, profile, and evidence boundaries.

## Review Gates

Major public reports, white papers, and top-result announcements require an
ethics/disclosure checklist before publication.

## Research Basis

NIST AI Risk Management Framework motivates transparency, validity,
accountability, and risk communication. Model Cards motivate documenting
intended use, limitations, evaluation context, and ethical considerations.
