# Benchmark-to-Research Pipeline

## Purpose

The benchmark-to-research pipeline defines how leaderboard activity becomes
credible analysis, reports, white papers, and reproducibility bundles.

## Pipeline Stages

1. Identify research question and hypothesis.
2. Define cohort, season, track, and exclusions.
3. Register analysis plan for major public claims when practical.
4. Export immutable dataset.
5. Run notebooks and technical report generator.
6. Conduct statistical and qualitative review.
7. Package charts, data, code, and artifact references.
8. Publish report, citation, and limitations.
9. Track corrections and independent replications.

## Required Artifacts

Research outputs include analysis manifest, data-export ID, notebook or script
versions, chart data, author list, conflict disclosures, and reproducibility
instructions.

## Separation From Leaderboard

Research claims may use leaderboard data, but they do not alter official ranks
unless a reanalysis policy explicitly applies.

## Research Basis

ACM artifact badging motivates available and evaluated artifacts for research
claims. FAIR principles and preregistration practice motivate reusable data,
clear hypotheses, and transparent analysis plans.
