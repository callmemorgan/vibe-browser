# Brand and Naming Guidelines

## Purpose

Brand and naming guidelines keep the benchmark recognizable, restrained, and
honest across the site, reports, badges, charts, and third-party references.

## Official Names

- Full name: `Vibe Browser Benchmark`
- Short name: `Vibe Browser`
- Avoid: `VibeBench`, `VBB`, or "the browser intelligence test" unless a
  future governance decision explicitly adopts the term.

## Terminology

Use "agent", "model", "harness", "provider", "season", "track", "profile",
"official-ranked", "official-unranked", "provisional", and "non-comparable"
consistently. Do not use "winner" without season and track.

## Claim Language

Allowed: "top official run in season X, track Y" and "frontier result for
profile Z." Avoid unsupported "state of the art", "best coding model",
"production-ready browser", or "human-level" claims.

## Visual Guidance

The brand should feel technical, clear, and public-interest oriented. Charts
prioritize readability over decoration. Use a color palette that supports
status distinction, colorblind-safe comparisons, and high-contrast text.

## Third-Party Use

Badges and embeds may use official naming only when they link to a stable run
or season page and preserve status labels. Vendors must not remove caveats from
official result cards.

## Research Basis

WCAG guidance motivates accessible contrast and non-color-only status
communication. NIST AI RMF transparency principles motivate claim boundaries
and clear communication of uncertainty.
